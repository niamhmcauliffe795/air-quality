Air Quality in New York City has Improved

Nitrogen Dioxide levels have dropped from 2012 to 2022 in NYC.

By Niamh McAuliffe
December 21, 2024

New York City air quality has improved in a decade. Photo by Niamh McAuliffe

In a decade, air quality improved in New York City with nitrogen dioxide levels dropping by almost 15% across the five boroughs. NO2 is an air pollutant that damages lung tissue and causes breathing problems, specifically people with asthma.

One of the main reasons for this drop is the regulations enforced in 2015 to phase out residual fuel oil in boilers, according to Columbia University Mailman School of Public Health. No. 6 fuel oil emits a very high amount of pollutants, including NO2.

However, NYC still does not meet the federal air quality standards.

“You can see the plume from New York City from space. It's huge, but it is much smaller than it used to be,” Róisín Commane, an Associate Professor of Earth and Environmental Sciences at Columbia University. “It's not small, but it is the cleanest it has ever been. That does not mean it's clean.”
